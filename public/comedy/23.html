OLD MAN: [sighs]
THERAPIST: So John, tell me what’s bothering you.
OLD MAN: I just feel so old, so disconnected from the young people of the world today. No matter what I try to say, they don’t listen to me. It’s almost as if I’m trying to speak to them through some invisible boundary that separates the generations.
THERAPIST: Please, tell me more about this boundary. Do you think it’s a psychological boundary that you have created in your own mind?
OLD MAN: I’d say it’s more physical than psychological. Also, it fogs up when I speak too close to it.
THERAPIST: John, are you referring to the front window of your house?
OLD MAN:...Why, yes. Yes I am.
